1. Introduction

This Agreement ("Agreement") is between the Owner/Agent and Tenants, collectively Parties. Both Parties agree to abide by the terms and conditions outlined for Premises provided in this Agreement Terms and Conditions section preceding the remainder of this Agreement, and as outlined in the sections below.

Agreement is binding on each individual who signs this Agreement, regardless of their actual residency status.

2. Applicable State Statutes & Act

This Agreement is governed by applicable Arkansas law, including the Arkansas Residential Landlord-Tenant Act of 2007, Ark. Code Ann. § 18-17-101 et seq., and Arkansas residential lease statutes including Ark. Code Ann. § 18-16-305 for security deposits. If a local ordinance or mandatory state law conflicts with this Agreement, the mandatory law controls.

3. Premises

In exchange for Rent Fee, Other Rent Fees, Move In Fees, and Other Fees during Lease Term specified in this Agreement, Owner/Agent hereby leases Premises to Tenant. The lease constituted in this Agreement does not include any other parts of the building where Premises is located unless explicitly stated in this Agreement.

4. Lease Term

This Agreement will commence and terminate on the dates specified under the Lease Term. Following this period, if this Agreement converts to a month-to-month tenancy, it will be under the same terms and conditions, except for any legal modifications, until terminated.

Tenant agrees to vacate Premises upon this Agreement's termination unless: (i) Owner/Agent and Tenant have agreed in writing to extend this Agreement or have entered into a new one; (ii) local rent control laws require otherwise; or (iii) Owner/Agent accepts rent from Tenant (excluding past due rent), which will create a month-to-month tenancy. This arrangement may be terminated by either party with 30 days' written notice by Tenant to Owner/Agent or by Owner/Agent to Tenant as legally required. The rent for the month-to-month tenancy will be set by mutual agreement or as permitted by law. All other terms and conditions of this Agreement will remain in full effect.

5. Rent

There will be no Rent increases during the initial Lease Term. Owner/Agent may increase Rent for any month-to-month renewal period by providing Tenant with at least 30 days' written notice. Owner/Agent's preferred method of payment is online through Hemlane. However, Tenant may make a payment via mail to Owner/Agent Address on or before the due date.

In the case of roommates in Premises or any form of joint or multiple occupancy, Tenant on this Agreement shall be responsible for collecting entire Rent and submitting one single payment to Owner/Agent. Tenant is responsible for ensuring that payments sent by mail or made online are received by the due date specified. Payments sent by mail must be received by Owner/Agent on or before the due date. Rent payments collected for any partial month will be pro-rated at a rate of 1/30th of the monthly rent per day. No pro-rated rent shall be accepted at any other time.

6. Security Deposit

At the time of signing this Agreement, Tenant must pay Security Deposit to Owner/Agent. Security Deposit may be used only as permitted by applicable law, including for unpaid rent, damages to Premises beyond ordinary wear and tear, cleaning or restoration costs allowed by this Agreement, and other lawful charges resulting from Tenant's noncompliance.

For Arkansas residential tenancies subject to Ark. Code Ann. § 18-16-305, Security Deposit may not exceed the statutory maximum, generally two (2) months' rent, and Owner/Agent must return the balance of Security Deposit and/or provide an itemized written statement of deductions within sixty (60) days after termination of the tenancy and delivery of possession, subject to the statute's scope and exceptions. Tenant must provide Owner/Agent with a forwarding address. If Tenant does not provide a forwarding address, Owner/Agent may send required notices and funds to Tenant's last known address as permitted by law.

Security Deposit is not prepaid rent and may not be applied to the last month's rent unless Owner/Agent agrees in writing or applicable law requires otherwise.

7. Late Fee

If Tenant fails to make the Rent payment on or before the initial late fee date, as outlined under Late Fee, Tenant will be responsible for paying Late Fee to Owner/Agent, in addition to overdue Rent and/or Other Fees, but only to the extent permitted by applicable law. Arkansas law does not impose a general residential late-fee cap in the same manner as some states, but any Late Fee must be authorized by this Agreement and should be reasonable in relation to Owner/Agent's collection costs and administrative burden.

Payment of a Late Fee does not cure the late payment for purposes of determining repeated or habitual lateness. Owner/Agent and Tenant agree that five (5) instances of late payment within any twelve (12) month period may be treated as habitual lateness and may constitute grounds for default or nonrenewal to the extent permitted by law.

Late rent fees, lease violation charges, attorney's/legal fees, and expenses related to enforcement of this Agreement may be treated as additional rent only to the extent permitted by applicable law. Owner/Agent may serve notices and pursue eviction or collection remedies only after satisfying any notice, cure, and court-process requirements under applicable law.

8. Management

Tenant is informed that Owner/Agent serves as the property manager for Premises. For any issues or concerns, Tenant can reach out to Owner/Agent. Depending on Owner/Agent's package on Hemlane, Tenant may have access to Hemlane rental assistants to answer questions and dispatch service professionals. Hemlane is not a property manager.

Owner/Agent requires Tenant(s) to be in possession of at least one working phone number at all times. It is Tenant(s) responsibility to provide Owner/Agent with prompt information regarding any changes to phone numbers and/or means of communication.

9. Non-Sufficient Funds

Tenant may incur a Non-Sufficient Funds Fee for each Rent payment returned due to insufficient funds, intended to cover the expenses incurred by Owner/Agent. Additionally, any returned check will be subject to Late Rent Fee provisions outlined in this Agreement (if applicable). These charges will be immediately due, and failure to pay promptly will be deemed a default under this Agreement.

In the event of a returned check, Owner/Agent reserves the right to require future payments to be made by cashier's check, money order, or certified funds. This does not limit the Owner/Agent's other remedies available for a dishonored check. Furthermore, Owner/Agent and Tenant agree that if three (3) checks are returned in any 12-month period due to insufficient funds, it may be deemed a frequent issue and could serve as just cause for eviction, if allowable by law.

10. Possession

Tenant shall gain possession of Premises on Lease Term Date and shall return possession to Owner/Agent on Lease End Date, unless Lease After End Date is month-to-month or both parties mutually agree otherwise in writing. Upon Lease Term expiration, Tenant will remove their belongings and peacefully return Premises to Owner/Agent in the same condition as when it was initially provided, with the exception of normal wear and tear.

11. Use of Premises

Tenant agrees to use Premises solely as their primary residence and not for any business, illegal, or hazardous activities.

The Premises is to be used only as a residence by the Tenants who have signed this Lease. NO OTHER PERSONS MAY LIVE AT THE PREMISES WITHOUT THE OWNER/AGENT'S PRIOR WRITTEN PERMISSION, which may be given or withheld in Owner/Agent's sole and absolute discretion. Guests may only stay for up to one week (7 days) and must abide by all applicable terms and conditions of this Lease, including any rules and regulations applicable to the Premises. Owner/Agent reserves the right to treat frequent visitors as unauthorized additional tenant(s).

The use of Premises for any retail, commercial, or professional purposes is prohibited unless Tenant obtains prior written consent from Owner/Agent and follows applicable zoning laws. If such use is approved, Tenant may be required to secure liability insurance for the protection of Owner/Agent. Owner/Agent reserves the right to deny approval for these activities at their discretion.

Tenant shall not use the Premises, nor any neighboring premises, for any illegal purpose, or for any other purpose than that of a residence. Tenant agrees to comply with and abide by all federal, state, county and municipal laws and ordinances in connection with Tenant’s occupancy and use of the Premises. No alcoholic beverages shall be possessed or consumed by Tenant, or Tenant’s family, visiting friends, dependents, guests, licensees or invitees, unless the person possessing or consuming alcohol is of legal age.

No illegal drugs or controlled substances (unless specifically prescribed by a physician for a specific person residing or present on the Premises) are permitted on the Premises. Tenant agrees to refrain from using the Premises in any way that may result in an increase of the rate or cost of insurance on the Premises. No hazardous or dangerous activities are permitted on the Premises. Absolutely no excessive drinking, illegal drug use, public disturbances, physical abuse, verbal abuse, threats, or unauthorized pets, firearms, or smoking is permitted on Premises.

Such violations of the foregoing clause shall constitute a material breach and may result in eviction proceedings pursuant to applicable law.

12. Nuisance & Waste

Tenant agrees not to engage in, or allow others to engage in, any acts of waste or nuisance on or around Premises. Waste refers to any damage or misuse that diminishes the value of Premises, while nuisance includes activities that significantly disrupt the comfort, safety, or enjoyment of the Owner/Agent, other occupants, neighbors, or their agents, guests, or invitees. Such acts are considered serious breaches of the tenancy agreement and may result in eviction proceedings.

OBJECTIONABLE CONDUCT – Tenant(s)will not engage in objectionable conduct. Objectionable conduct means behavior, which makes or will make the apartment or the property less fit to live in for other Tenant(s). It also means anything that interferes with the right of others to enjoy life in their Unit(s) in peace, or causes conditions that are dangerous, hazardous, unsanitary, and detrimental to Tenant(s) at the premises and/or adjacent properties.

NOISE – Tenant(s), their families, guests/visitors should not make or be permitted to make any disturbing noises in the Unit or at the property or permitted to do anything that may interfere with the right, comforts, or convenience of other tenants or neighboring premises. Tenant may not infringe upon the quiet enjoyment right of other tenants through disturbances including but not limited to TVs, stereos, musical instruments, other loud noises, heavy walking, or other disturbing actions.

PETS – Dogs, cats or other animals or pets are not permitted at the property without Owner/Agent approval. If permitted, at Owner/Agent's discretion, a Tenant(s) must agree to Pet Policy and Pet Policy Agreement provisions including required deposits and/or fees. The granting of consent for pets to others in properties under management by Owner/Agent shall in no way be considered the granting of consent to Tenant.

Tenant is responsible for keeping a pet from disturbing other Tenants or allowing a nuisance such chronic noise, failure to properly dispose of pet waste, and having their pets unsupervised.

Complaints regarding failure to comply with this Policy must be made in writing to management. If the complaint is determined to be valid, management will issue a warning to the tenant who must immediately remedy the situation.

A Tenant who fails to remedy the situation after Second Warning will receive a Three-Day Notice to put out for board or otherwise remove the pet from the leased premises for the balance of the Lease term.

Threatening behavior, animal bites, attacks, or if the pet becomes a nuisance or annoyance to neighbors will constitute grounds for immediate removal. If the Tenant fails to remove the pet after receiving notice to do so, Tenant will be considered to be in breach of his/her Lease and may be evicted, and if so, will forfeit any Pet Security Deposit.

Owner/Agent will make every effort to keep the property safe and secure. Tenant(s) further understands and acknowledges that these efforts do not create a Promise of Security.

14. Absences & Abandonment

Tenant is required to notify Owner/Agent in writing of any anticipated absence from Premises lasting longer than 14 days, no later than the first day of the absence.

When Tenant is absent from Premises for an extended period, Tenant is required to exercise sound judgment to maintain the HVAC system at a reasonable temperature and ensure that all windows are securely closed in order to prevent potential water damage, freezing of pipes, and other possible damages. Failure to comply with this requirement may result in liability for any resulting damage to Premises.

The Premises will be deemed abandoned if Tenant defaults in rent payment, appears absent from the Premises, and there is reason to believe that Tenant will not be returning to the Premises, as determined by Owner/Agent in its reasonable discretion. Should the Premises be considered abandoned, Owner/Agent will take possession immediately, change all locks, and store Tenant’s personal property items, at Tenant’s expense (to the extent Owner/Agent is required to do so by applicable laws).

If Tenant(s) vacates the Leased Premises before the end of the Lease term without written permission from the Owner/Agent, the Leased Premises will be considered to be abandoned and Tenant(s) to be in default of the Lease Agreement. Tenant(s) found in such default will be responsible for damages and losses allowed by federal, state, and local law.

If one of the Tenants under this Lease dies before the end of the Lease term, any remaining Tenants shall continue to carry out the terms of the Lease. If the deceased Tenant is the sole Tenant under the Lease, a representative of the deceased Tenant may terminate this Lease by providing verified written documentation testifying to such Tenant’s death.

If, after the Lease's Term legal expiration, any personal items are left at the premises without written communication with Owner/Agent, all personal items will be considered to be abandoned. In such event, Owner/Agent may either discard the property or store it at tenants' expense; Tenant(s) will be responsible to Owner/Agent for all costs and expenses incurred by removal of such items.

Owner/Agent shall have no liability to Tenant whatsoever in connection with the storage of any of Tenant’s personal property beyond what is required by applicable law. Tenant shall indemnify, defend and hold Owner/Agent harmless from and against any and all penalties, damages, fines, causes of action, liabilities, judgments, expenses (including, without limitation, attorneys’ fees) or charges incurred in connection with or arising from Owner/Agent's storage of Tenant’s personal property.
